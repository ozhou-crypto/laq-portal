Collection notice and privacy statement

Version 2.1 — Updated 01 Mar 2026

Summary in plain English

Legal Aid Queensland (LAQ) collects personal and practice information about you through this portal to administer grants of legal aid on behalf of your clients. We will use this information to process applications, manage payments, and communicate with you about matters you handle. We will not disclose your information to third parties except as required by law or with your consent. You have the right to access and correct information we hold about you by contacting LAQ.

Full collection notice

1. Purpose of collection

Legal Aid Queensland ABN 49 846 898 098 collects personal information through this portal for the purpose of administering the grant of legal aid scheme under the Legal Aid Queensland Act 1997 (Qld). This includes assessing applications, managing grants, processing disbursements, and maintaining the integrity of the scheme.

Information is also collected to verify your identity, confirm your practising credentials, manage your portal access and licence allocations, and to comply with obligations under the Information Privacy Act 2009 (Qld).

2. What we collect

We may collect your full name, preferred contact details, law practice details, ABN, banking details for payment purposes, Queensland Law Society or Bar Association membership number, and matter-specific information you provide when lodging applications or submitting correspondence through the portal.

We also collect technical information such as login timestamps, IP addresses, session identifiers, and audit log records of actions taken within the portal. This information is used solely for security monitoring and compliance purposes.

3. How we use it

Your information is used to process and determine applications for legal aid, to communicate with you about matters you manage, to make payments for approved work, and to generate reporting required by LAQ's funding agreements and legislation.

We may use de-identified aggregated data for statistical and policy purposes. We will not use your information for direct marketing.

4. Disclosure

LAQ may disclose your personal information to the Queensland Law Society, the Bar Association of Queensland, the Queensland Civil and Administrative Tribunal, or courts where disclosure is required by law or is necessary to administer a grant of aid. We may also disclose information to approved service providers who assist us in operating this portal, under confidentiality agreements.

LAQ does not disclose personal information outside Australia except where you have expressly consented or where disclosure is required by an applicable law.

5. Your rights

You have the right to access personal information we hold about you and to request corrections if the information is inaccurate, out of date, or incomplete. To make an access or correction request, contact the LAQ Privacy Officer at privacy@legalaid.qld.gov.au or by post to GPO Box 2449, Brisbane QLD 4001.

You may also lodge a privacy complaint with the Office of the Information Commissioner Queensland. Details are available at www.oic.qld.gov.au.
