Design approach OOTB first · SLDS 2.0 · WCAG 2.2 AA LAQ Practitioner Portal

Three principles we've held to

These came from the budget conversation in December. They've shaped every screen that follows.

1. OOTB first

Every region of every screen asked: can a standard Experience Cloud component do this? If yes, we use it, even when a bespoke design would be prettier. Custom LWCs are flagged and justified in each handoff spec.

2. SLDS 2.0 baseline, LAQ brand override

We keep SLDS 2.0 type, spacing, buttons and corner radii untouched. LAQ brand is applied as Experience Cloud theme only: logo, primary colour #05325F, and a system sans-serif.

3. WCAG 2.2 AA, plain Australian English

Status is conveyed by icon and text, never colour alone. Visible focus states. 4.5:1 body contrast. No marketing language, no emojis, no celebratory imagery on government service screens.

Sign-in (MFA) — external practitioners

Primary entry point for Preferred Suppliers, CLCs, barristers, FDRPs and support staff. Password plus a 6-digit code, with a 30-day device trust option.
